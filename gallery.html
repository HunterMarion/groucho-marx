Rouge's Gallery

Mugshot

In this portrait, we can see his iconic mustache, eyebrows, glasses, and cigar combo. Originally only a costume choice for his characters on stage, Marx eventually adopted it as his own personal look later in life.

Brothers-in-Arms

Groucho's younger brother, Harpo Marx often played the silly, silent foil to Groucho's loud, wise-cracking characters. The two remained close throughout their entire lives.

Laidback

Like most comedic actors during the pre- and post-war period, Groucho's antics tended to be oriented around slapstick or oddball behavior. This exact film would later on inspire Freddie Mercury to create the renowned Queen album of the same name.

Waddya Lookin' At?

The Marx Brother's career began when the brothers started heckling a crowd of hecklers back during a shoddy singing performance. They stopped singing ever since.

Ole' Pals

Groucho Marx was close with many stars, including Alice Cooper, Elton John, and Dick Cavett. Elton John's album Don't Shoot Me, I'm Only the Piano Player was named after an exchange Marx had with John while drinking together.

Birdie

A master of subtlty and brutish humor, Marx encapsulated the strange humor of turn-of-the-century men. Simultaneously sophisticated and boorish; relatable but ridiculous.
